Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 - What’s new for 2025

The Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 (ECCTA) has introduced wide ranging changes including new powers for Companies House and the upcoming failure to prevent fraud offence, as well as strict new identity verification requirements for directors and others.

In this session, we will recap the key changes introduced by ECCTA and explore the new Authorised Corporate Service Provider registration requirements and identity verification requirements. We will also take a look at the forthcoming failure to prevent fraud offence and highlight any key action points for accountants, firms and companies for smooth implementation of ECCTA requirements.

By registering for this webinar, you will:

  • Get an overview of the changes introduced by the Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 including the amendments to the accounts filing requirements;
  • Understand why, when and how to register as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider;
  • Identity verification requirements for directors, LLP members and PSCs - how to carry out identity verification;
  • Understand the background and the key features of the new failure to prevent fraud offence; and
  • Gain key action points for smooth ECCTA requirements implementation.
